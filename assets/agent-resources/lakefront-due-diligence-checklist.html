Lakefront Due-Diligence Checklist

Everything to verify before your buyer writes an offer on the water.

Print one per property. Working through this with your buyer prevents post-inspection surprises and positions you as the specialist who protects them.

Water & shoreline

  • Frontage width confirmed (survey, not listing) and bottom type walked (sand / rock / muck)
  • Swimmable depth off the dock; weed growth and any invasive species (milfoil, zebra mussels)
  • Exposure and wind fetch; erosion, rip-rap condition, high-water marks
  • Dock type (permanent vs. seasonal) — is it permitted and conveying? Lift included?
  • Water level history and any DNR/ordinary-high-water-mark considerations

Land, setbacks & buildability

  • County shoreland ordinance: setbacks, impervious-surface limits, bluff/steep-slope rules
  • Any non-conforming structures? Confirm what can be rebuilt/remodeled and what's grandfathered
  • Survey and staked boundaries; easements (shared access, utility, plat)
  • Flood zone status and insurance implications

Systems (the expensive surprises)

  • Septic: type, age, compliance certificate, last pump/inspection — replacement on a lake lot is costly
  • Well: potability + flow test; distance to septic; shared-well agreements
  • Heat & insulation: is it truly year-round? Heat tape on lines, crawlspace, winterization
  • Electrical & power reliability: generator/backup for storm outages; boathouse wiring

Access, use & ownership

  • Road: public vs. private; who maintains and plows in winter; association dues
  • Lake association rules, boat/dock restrictions, no-wake zones, public-access traffic nearby
  • Rental restrictions (if short-term income is part of the plan)
  • Property taxes (verify current + likely reassessment) and homestead vs. seasonal classification

Documents to collect

  • Survey · Septic compliance · Well record · Seller's disclosure · Association docs/bylaws
  • Any permits for dock, shoreline work, additions · DNR lake survey (depth, clarity, fishery)
