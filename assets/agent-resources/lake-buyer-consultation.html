Lake Buyer Consultation Guide

The questions that turn a "just looking" buyer into a focused, loyal one.

Lake buyers are emotional and often out-of-area. A great consultation saves everyone months of aimless showings and makes you the expert they trust. Run this before you send a single listing.

1. The dream (get them talking)

  • "Paint me a perfect Saturday at the lake — who's there, what are you doing?"
  • "Is this a weekend cabin, a future retirement home, or year-round now?"
  • "Fishing, watersports, quiet mornings with coffee, or entertaining a crowd?"
Why Use vs. lake type is everything. A watersports family wants big, clear, deep water; a fisherman may want a shallow walleye lake; a quiet retiree wants a small no-wake lake. Match the buyer to the water, then the house.

3. Budget & financing reality

  • "What's your comfortable all-in monthly, including taxes and insurance?" (lake taxes + insurance surprise people).
  • Second-home / non-owner-occupied loans differ — are they pre-approved for a seasonal property?
  • Cash vs. financed changes strategy in a fast lake market.
  • Set expectations on carrying costs: point them to the true-cost-of-ownership guide.

4. Set expectations (protect the relationship)

You: "Good lake homes on the right water move fast — sometimes in days. My job is to have you pre-approved, clear on your must-haves, and ready to see the right one quickly so we don't lose it. In exchange, I'll be honest when a place is wrong for you, even if it's beautiful."

5. Educate on lake-specific risks (builds trust fast)

  • Shoreland rules & setbacks — what they can and can't build/remodel near the water.
  • Septic & well — age, compliance, and replacement cost on a lake lot.
  • Shoreline type — sand vs. muck vs. rip-rap and what it means for use and value.
  • Association / lake access rules, DNR public accesses, and summer traffic.
