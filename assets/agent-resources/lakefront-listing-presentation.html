Lakefront Listing Presentation Script

Win the listing by proving you understand water, not just houses.

Selling a lake home is a different sale than a city house — buyers pay for the shoreline, water, and lifestyle as much as the structure. This script positions you as the specialist who prices and markets all of it. Adapt the blanks to the property.

1. Open — set the frame (2 min)

You: "Thanks for having me out to . Before we talk price, I want to walk the shoreline with you — because on a lake, the frontage is often worth more than the house. My whole business is Minnesota lake property, so let's make sure we market what actually drives the value here."
Why it works You immediately separate yourself from a generalist agent and anchor the conversation on the thing buyers pay a premium for.

2. Walk the water — the value audit (10 min)

Take notes out loud. Buyers and sellers both respect an agent who sees what they miss:

  • Frontage & bottom: feet of shoreline, sand vs. rock vs. muck, swimmable depth, weed line.
  • Exposure: which way it faces (west = sunsets = premium), wind fetch, privacy.
  • Dock & access: permanent vs. seasonal dock, lift, slope to water, stairs.
  • Water body: lake size, clarity, fishery, public access traffic, no-wake zones.
  • Land: elevation, buildable area, shoreland setbacks, septic/well age.

3. Price — anchor on shoreline, not just comps (8 min)

You: "Most agents price a lake home off the house and square footage. I price it three ways — comparable sold lake homes, price per foot of frontage, and lot value if it were vacant land. On , that points to a range of to . Here's the data behind each."
Pro move Bring the DNR lake facts (depth, clarity, fishery) and recent sold comps for that specific lake. Pair this with the Waterfront CMA & Pricing Worksheet resource.

4. Market — where lake buyers actually look (5 min)

  • Featured placement on the lake's own page at MinnesotaLakeHomesForSale.com, where buyers researching that exact lake land.
  • Professional water-side photos + drone; shoreline and sunset shots, not just interiors.
  • Distribution to the metro and out-of-state second-home buyers who drive up lake prices.
  • A dedicated property page you can share to your database and socials.

5. Close — ask for the listing (2 min)

You: "You want the person selling your home to know this lake bay by bay — where the sand is, why the west exposure matters, and who's buying here right now. That's what I do all day. If you're comfortable, let's get it priced right and in front of lake buyers this week. Shall we?"
